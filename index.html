Smart Entry System

Drive in without stopping.

For the Primecore Elite package, members can enter via app pass or simply drive up — our system recognizes their license plate and opens the gate automatically.

  • License Plate Recognition (LPR) Cameras at the lane read the plate, match it to an active membership, and open the gate — no phone required.
  • Rotating QR Code A secure, time-limited code refreshes every 60 seconds — scanned at your entrance kiosk or lane reader.
  • Multi-Vehicle Support Members register one or more plates on their account — ideal for families and fleet plans.
  • Live Membership Check Gate only opens for active members — expired or paused accounts are blocked automatically.
  • Works With Your Hardware We integrate with standard LPR cameras, barrier controllers, and access systems common in car wash installs.

FAQ

Common questions.

How does license plate recognition work?

Members add their plate(s) in the app during signup or anytime after. On Elite, an LPR camera at the entrance reads the plate, our system verifies an active membership in real time, and the gate opens — typically in under a second. Plates can also be looked up manually in the admin dashboard if a read fails.

Do I need special hardware for the gate system?

Most car washes already have a barrier controller. We integrate via API with common systems (IoT relays, LPR cameras, standard access controllers). If you need new readers, we can recommend compatible hardware — typically $800–$2,500 per lane depending on setup.

What if we open before the app is ready?

We prioritize a "soft launch" core — membership sign-up and admin tools — by week 5, so you can start enrolling members even while gate integration is being finalized (Elite tier).

Can we change membership plans later?

Yes. Your admin dashboard lets you create, edit, and retire plans without developer involvement. Price changes sync to Stripe automatically.

Who owns the code?

You receive full ownership of all custom code upon final payment — no licensing fees or recurring platform charges.

What about App Store fees?

Apple and Google developer accounts (~$99/yr and $25 one-time) are standard industry costs and can be set up under your business name at launch.
